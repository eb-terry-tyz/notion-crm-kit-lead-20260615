## Airtable Tables To Export First

Before importing anything into Notion, export the Airtable tables that represent the actual operating workflow:

- Contacts and companies, including email, phone, source, owner, and notes.
- Deals or opportunities, including stage, value, close date, next action, and linked company.
- Clients and projects, including status, kickoff date, delivery owner, and active milestones.
- Meetings and follow-ups, including date, related client, decision, and next task.
- Invoices or payment tracking fields if those are already part of your operating system.

Cleaning these CSV files before import matters more than the template itself. Duplicate company names, inconsistent stage labels, stale close dates, and blank owners will make the Notion CRM harder to use after migration.

## How The Notion CSV Import Workflow Works

1. Export each Airtable table as CSV and keep the original files unchanged as a backup.
2. Normalize dropdown values such as lead source, pipeline stage, project status, and owner.
3. Import the CSV files into Notion as databases, then assign the intended property types.
4. Recreate CRM and business OS views for active pipeline, client delivery, meetings, follow-ups, and weekly review.
5. Test the rebuilt workspace with a small set of real records before moving all operating work into Notion.

The kit helps with the structure and field decisions so you are not designing every CRM page from a blank Notion workspace.
